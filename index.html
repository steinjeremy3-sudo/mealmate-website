The inefficiency

Empty seats, every week.

Most independent restaurants run at 30–40% capacity on weeknights and slow lunch shifts. Discounting is the obvious solution — and the obvious mistake. Public coupons train regulars to wait. Reservation discounts force restaurants onto a marketplace they don't control. Delivery apps take 30% and don't fill chairs.

The result: restaurants either tolerate the empty seats or take painful steps that hurt their brand. Neither is good.

MealMate is a card-linked discount platform that lets independent restaurants fill their slow hours — and gives their best diners a way to dine more often, for less.

How it works

A small loop. No coupons. No POS changes.

01

The restaurant sets a discount.

Pick the daypart, pick the percentage, pick the budget cap. Pause anytime. Existing POS keeps running.

02

The diner pays normally.

Linked card, normal payment. The discount applies at the moment of payment — no codes, no QR, no waiting.

03

The restaurant pays nothing.

Zero cost to the restaurant. A small platform fee is absorbed by the diner on the discounted total.

The discount lives at the point of payment, not on a coupon site.

Why now

Three shifts converging on the same product shape.

01

Operators need measurable demand.

Cost pressure has eliminated tolerance for generic awareness spend. Restaurants want spend that ties to incremental covers, not impressions.

02

Card-linked rails matured.

The infrastructure for card-linked offers exists today in a way it didn't five years ago. Toast, Resy, and others have made the underlying plumbing real.

03

Diners already browse digitally.

OpenTable serves 60,000+ restaurants and ingests 400,000+ reviews per month. Discovery is digital. Rewards aren't.
